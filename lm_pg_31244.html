Grammar: more past tenses

What other tenses are used to talk about the past?

We looked at past simple, past continuous and present perfect - the tenses we use most often to talk about the past. But in addition to these tenses, there is also
  • past perfect
    • used to show that one event took place before another one in the past
Past perfect also has a continous form that is used to emphasise the continous aspect to show that one event was in progress before another one in the past.

If you feel confident enough with these tenses and think you can use them correctly, you can skip ahead to the comparison with past simple below and try some practice exercises.
If you need more information and practice, click on the panels below.
Past perfect
When and how do we use the past perfect tense?

Hint! The past perfect is very similar to the Estonian tense 'enneminevik' (olin teinud) in its form as well as use!

We use the past perfect to show that one event happened before another one in the past.
E.g. I had finished the sandwich when Tom arrived with a pizza.
