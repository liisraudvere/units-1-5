Vocabulary and expressions
How do we talk about habits?

Below are some phrases we can use to talk about our habits:
  • I tend to wake up rather early on weekdays.
  • I have the tendency to avoid people in the mornings.
  • I keep checking the news throughout the day.
  • I am always forgetting my phone on silent.
  • I have a habit of skipping breakfast.
  • I am used to drinking a cup of coffee first thing in the morning.
  • In the evenings I will often just binge watch Netflix.
  • I have gotten into the habit of going out to lunch.
  • I am in the habit of falling asleep very late.
There are also some useful expressions about habits:
  • If something is a force of habit you do it without even thinking about it - you do it by the force of habit.
  • Old habits die hard means it is difficult to break a habit.
  • If you are a creature of habit, you do something because you are used to it.
  • If you kick a habit you stop doing something you are used to doing.
  • If you are trying to get into the habit of doing something, you are trying to start doing something regularly.
