Conjunctions

How can I make my writing better? How can I write more complex sentences? 

In the following chapter you will learn how to write longer and more complex sentences. Tying shorter and simpler sentences into more complex ones makes your work more interesting and pleasant to follow as well as shows your higher level of language.
 
The most basic way of combining sentences is using conjunctions (and, but, because etc.), which are used for connecting ideas, words, clauses etc. Conjunctions are basically used for joining parts of sentences.

List of conjunctions
List of the most common conjunctions in alphabetical order.

after - They went home after completing the task.
although - He game me the letter although he was told not to.
and - My friends and relatives all came to the party.
as - I tried to contact him as it was very important that he knew.
as if - The people were screaming as if their lives were in danger.
as long as - We are willing to help you as long as we get paid.
as though - He looked at me as though he did not know who I was.
because - I wanted to be there on time, because it was really important.
before - We had already heard the news before they came.
both / and - I am interested in both history and law.
but - We wanted to speak to him, but he was already gone.
either / or - He told me to bring either food or drinks.
even if - Even if he brings the documents, it is still not enough.
even though - Even though I like you very much, I cannot tell you the secret.
for - He was very surprised to see the result, for he was not expecting it.
however - They arrived late; however, they did not miss anything.
if - I will let him know if i see him.
in case - I want to leave early in case there are long queues.
neither / nor - He likes neither tea nor coffee.
not only / but also - Not only was it huge, but also expensive.
once - We will start working once we receive the final orders.
or - You can bring me letter yourself or send it by mail.
since - He did not finish the job since he did not get paid for that.
so - Come to the party tomorrow, so we can get to know each other better!
so that - You must finish your part so that I could start mine.
than - We have much less to do today than we had yesterday.
that - We all that it was a great idea.
though - I wanted to see him, though I was quite busy.
unless - We will go to the party unless something else comes up.
until - I will keep calling him until he picks up.
when - We saw him when we were crossing the street.
where - The house where I live was built in the 90s.
whereas - It took him four hours, whereas I did it in one hour.
whether - I don't know whether he is coming.
whether / or - He asked me whether I would like to stay or go.
while - We were all waiting outside while they finished their argument.
yet - They wanted me to to the job, yet they did not want to pay me.
