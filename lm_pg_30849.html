Grammar: present tenses

What tenses do we use to talk about the present?


When in Estonian we only have one tense that we use to talk about the present, in English there are two:
  • the present simple to talk about habits and routines, things that happen regularly and are always true - I WORK IN THE EDF.
  • the present continuous to talk about things happening at the moment of speaking or temporary activities - I AM WORKING ON A PROJECT.
You can jump right to the end of the page to study the review and try the linked exercises, if you feel confident enough.
If you need more help, both tenses are explained in more detail below.
Present simple
How and when do we use the present simple?

We use the present simple to talk about things that
  • happen regularly 
  • are facts / always true

Present continuous
When and how do we use the present continuous?

The present continuous is used to talk about
  • actions happening at the moment of speaking (I'm cleaning my rifle = while I am talking to you)
  • temporary activities (He is teaching at the Military Academy = maybe not right now, but it is a temporary thing, not something he does all the time)
  • continuing changes (The average salary in EDF is increasing = in general, not specifically right now, but the situation is changing) 
  • annoying habits (usually with the word 'always' - You are always leaving your uniform on the chair = I am annoyed by the fact that you keep doing that)

How do we know which present tense to use?

The first question you should ask yourself when choosing which tense to use is WHEN? What is the time you are talking about? The past, present or future?

If it is the present, do you want to say that something happens all the time, regularly? Or do you want to say that it is going on right now?
