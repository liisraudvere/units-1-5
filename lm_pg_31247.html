Characteristics of formal and informal writing
What are the features of formal and informal letters?

The following are the main differences between formal and informal letters you should keep in mind:

Informal: contractions (can’t, won’t, I’m, etc.)
Formal: full forms (cannot, will not, I am, etc.)

Informal: idiomatic expressions, slang (mate, cool)
Formal: standard, neutral vocabulary (friend, wonderful)

Informal: direct questions (Where is the hotel?)
Formal: indirect questions (Could you tell me where the hotel is located?)

Informal: simple, everyday vocabulary (chat, start)
Formal: academic, sophisticated vocabulary (conversation, commence)

Informal: phrasal verbs (put off; look into)
Formal: single verb phrases used (postpone; study)

Informal: colloquial, everyday expressions (I’m writing about…)
Formal: set formal expressions (I am contacting you with regard to…)

Informal: ellipsis (leaving out words), simplified sentence structure (Sorry I missed it. The woman I saw…)
Formal: full, more complex sentences (I regret missing the event. The woman, who I saw…)

Informal: active voice (Your secretary sent me an e-mail.)
Formal: occasional passive voice (An e-mail sent by your secretary.)

Informal: personal tone (I think it’s a good idea)
Formal: impersonal tone (It could be a great idea)

Informal: simple linking devices (but, and, then)
Formal: formal linking devices (however, additionally, afterwards)

Informal: prepositions at the end (Who am I talking to?)
Formal: prepositions in the beginning (To whom am I talking?)

Informal: may use imperative voice (Remember…)
Formal: avoids imperative voice (Please keep in mind…)

Try to remember these differences in order to make your writing more effective and proper.
